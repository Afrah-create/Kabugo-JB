About the artist

Kabugo John Bosco is a Ugandan visual artist whose practice explores drawing, sculpture, and photography as interconnected forms of expression. His work focuses on human identity, emotion, and visual storytelling through careful observation and creative interpretation.

Bosco studied Fine Art at the Margareta Trowell School of Industrial and Fine Arts at Makerere University, where he built strong foundations in drawing, painting, sculpture, and visual design. Through portrait drawing and figurative studies, he investigates human expression and character, using line, tone, and form to reveal emotional depth. His sculptural works extend these explorations into three-dimensional space; photography serves as both documentation and inquiry into everyday life.

“Ultimately, my work seeks to demonstrate that art is not only a form of expression but also a powerful tool for storytelling, identity, and transformation.”

— Kabugo John Bosco

Artist statement

My artistic practice explores the relationship between creativity, identity, and human experience through drawing, sculpture, and photography. I am interested in how visual art can communicate emotion, cultural narratives, and personal stories that resonate with diverse audiences. Influenced by contemporary African artistic expression, my work reflects a dialogue between tradition, modern creativity, and personal interpretation.

As the founder of JBALLER CREATIONZ, I extend my artistic vision into creative design and visual communication for individuals, institutions, and communities.

Practice & disciplines

From studio walls to schools and brands — a practice rooted in observation, material, and design.

  • Drawing & portraiture

    Figurative studies and commissioned portraits in charcoal, pencil, and mixed drawing media.

  • Sculpture

    Three-dimensional forms that extend line and volume into space and presence.

  • Photography

    Composition and narrative — documenting life and reframing the everyday.

  • Painting

    Oil and acrylic painting alongside expressive colour and surface exploration.

  • Murals

    Large-scale wall work for schools, organisations, and public-facing spaces.

  • Graphic design

    Logos, branding, and visual systems through JBALLER CREATIONZ.
